Attractions

Dhyanalinga

Just sitting silently for a few minutes within the sphere of Dhyanalinga is enough to make even those unaware of meditation experience a state of deep meditativeness.

Naga Shrine

The sacred serpent is significant in the spiritual process because of its ability to be still and its extraordinary sense of perception, especially for those seeking to explore mysticism.

Trimoorti Panel

The Trimurti Panel located behind the Dhyanalinga depicts the three fundamental qualities essential for one’s inner growth: Rudra, Hara, and Sadashiva.

Sarpavasal

The gate has a beautiful carving of a cobra on its central pillar, symbolizing the snake’s heightened perception and its significance for spiritual seekers.

Kalabhairava

Kalabhairava is an intense form of Shiva that is beyond time. On the auspicious night of Phalguna Purnima, Sadhguru established Kalabhairava at Isha Yoga Center Coimbatore, marking one stage of the larger Kalabhairava consecration.

Teerthakunds

In India, it is a tradition to wet the entire body to enhance one’s receptivity before entering a sacred space. The Isha Yoga Center houses two Teerthakunds – the Suryakund for men and the Chandrakund for women.

Gaushala

The cow is revered and seen as a symbol of wealth, strength, abundance, selfless giving, and a prosperous life. The Gaushalas at the Isha Yoga Center are an important step in preserving native breeds of cows.

Adiyogi Divya Darshanam

A powerful 14-minute video imaging show projected on the 112-ft Adiyogi. Accompanied by a narration on Adiyogi in Sadhguru’s voice, the spellbinding show is a major attraction, depicting Adiyogi’s contribution and offering to humanity.

112 Ft Adiyogi

Adiyogi, transmitted the science of Yoga to the Saptarishis. He expounded 112 ways through which human beings can transcend their limitations and reach their ultimate potential.

Linga Bhairavi

Those who earn the Grace of Bhairavi neither have to live in concern or fear of life or death, of poverty, or of failure. All that human beings consider as wellbeing will be theirs, if only they earn the Grace of Bhairavi.

Isha Shop

Isha Life endeavors to bring this timeless Yogic wisdom into your daily life through carefully crafted offerings that enable you to Live Consciously.

Nada Aradhana

An offering of sound to the Dhyanalinga, it features an etheric blend of flowing vocals, singing bowls, drums and various other instruments amplified within the reverberating Dome, which enhances one’s receptivity to the energies of Dhyanalinga.

About Your Guide

The consecrated spaces of the Isha Yoga Center—especially Dhyanalinga, Adiyogi, and the powerful energy fields—became a turning point, revealing dimensions of well-being and spiritual depth that no achievement in the material world could match.

No prior yoga or meditation experience is needed—just an open heart and a sincere longing for deeper connection. Let me accompany you to make your time at Isha truly life-enhancing.

FAQs

1. What is the exact duration and group size for the trip?

This is a 2-day / 3-night spiritual excursion at the Isha Yoga Center in Coimbatore (typically aligned with long weekends or auspicious periods). The group is intentionally small—limited to 15 participants—to ensure a personalized, immersive experience with focused guidance.

2. What is included in the program cost, and what is excluded?

Inclusions typically cover: twin-sharing AC cottage accommodation, two satvik vegetarian meals per day (brunch & dinner), airport pickup/drop-off (from Coimbatore Airport, ~40 km away), guided tours/activities by experienced Isha Hatha Yoga teachers, and access to key spiritual experiences (e.g., Dhyanalinga meditation, Teerthakund dips, Adiyogi Light & Sound Show, Devi Abhishekam, Nada Aradhana, Aumkar meditation initiation). Exclusions include: flights/travel to Coimbatore, personal expenses, any optional shopping, and travel insurance. Exact pricing and payment details will be shared upon inquiry.

3. Is prior yoga or meditation experience required to join?

No prior experience is needed. The trip is open to beginners and seasoned practitioners alike. It's designed for anyone seeking a meaningful break, stress relief, self-care, inner connection, or a deeper taste of yogic spirituality. Guidance will be provided to make practices accessible.

4. What are the accommodation and meal details?

Accommodation is in comfortable twin-sharing air-conditioned cottages on the Isha Yoga Center premises. Meals are wholesome, satvik (pure vegetarian, yogic) food served at the center's dining halls—two main meals per day. Meals are eaten in silence to support mindfulness.

5. Are there any health or age restrictions for participating?

The program involves light physical activities (e.g., yoga, walking around the center, optional dips in Teerthakund). It is generally suitable for most adults in reasonable health. Pregnant women, those with serious medical conditions, or mobility issues should consult a doctor first and inform the organizers in advance. There is no strict upper age limit, but participants should be able to manage moderate walking and ashram routines.

6. What is the cancellation and refund policy?

We do not offer refunds under any circumstances. Travel insurance is recommended to cover unforeseen issues.

7. Will I have free time, or is the schedule fully packed?

The schedule balances guided activities (meditation sessions, consecrated space visits, group discussions) with personal time for reflection, self-paced exploration, or rest. Daily routines allow space to absorb the energy of the center without feeling rushed.

8. Can I extend my stay at the Isha Yoga Center after the program ends?

Yes, many participants choose to stay longer independently. You can request additional stay at Isha, subject to availability. The group organizers can guide you on how to arrange this.

9. What is the dress code or etiquette at the Isha Yoga Center?

Modest, comfortable clothing is recommended (e.g., salwar/kurta, loose pants, t-shirts; avoid revealing or tight clothes). Remove shoes before entering consecrated spaces like Dhyanalinga or temples. Maintain silence in meditation areas, respect the energy of the space, and follow ashram guidelines shared on arrival.

10. How do I register, and what is the next step?

Express interest via the website/contact form/WhatsApp. You'll receive details on dates, cost, itinerary, and payment. Spots fill quickly due to the small group size, so early registration is advised.
